Hi, I'm Omkar Vengurlekar

Second Year Ph.D. Student
Computer Engineering at Arizona State University

I am a Second-year Ph.D. student in Computer Engineering at Arizona State University. My research interests lie in computer vision, computer graphics, parallel-computing, and deep learning. I am passionate about developing robust and efficient algorithms for real-world applications. I specialize in designing algorithms for Implicit Neural representation, Novel View Synthesis, Volumetric Rendering and 3D Reconstruction. I am particularly interested in developing solutions for complex computer vision challenges using deep learning, computational imaging and rendering techniques.

I have gained valuable research experience through visiting Dartmouth College (Computer Science) as a visiting Researcher, where I worked on brainstorming and developing novel algorithms for None-Line-of-Sight Imaginging and 3D Reconstruction. I have also gained industry experience through insternship at Rain AI where I worked on developing efficient algorithms using GPU based parallel computing which helps me to developing robust and low latency algorithms for rendering and image processing applications.
